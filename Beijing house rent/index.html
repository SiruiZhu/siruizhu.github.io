A sketch of Beijing's House Rent

BY Sirui Zhu

Published on 31th August

It is quite a hot topic in China now. The problem is not specific to Beijing. According to Global Times, rising demands have led to an increase in rent princes in first-tier cities across China. Putting young people under more pressure.
According to the news, the average Beijing rent was RMB 91.5(USD 13) per square meter, with a 2.2 percent month-on-month rise.
People on social media in China commented:"I cannot afford to buy, cannot afford to rent, cannot afford to die - it leaves us with nothing at all."
This story simply have a insight of what is house rent in Beijing looks like, what might be the key factors when comes to rent price.

Better decoration worth more?

There are many house agency companies in China, the biggest one might be Lianjia, with there appartment renting brand Ziroom. Ziroom has decorations of shared apartment, which is popular with young people. You can move in immediately.
Agencies will also have some decorations when taking a house from landlord, which are in the profits for agencies. Here is the price might look when a house/apaprtment is decorated.
I scraped about 3000 houses avaliable for August on Lianjia website, have some analysis.

When renting an entire house, house with exquisite decoration are more expensive.

I used Baidu API to get the coordinates, and used a mapping tool to get the heat map.
Not surprisingly, the city center is more expensive than suburb.
As far as I know, people won't rent an apartment that is located in central district, and because of the pricy rent, people are more likely to live in suburb and using public transport to commute.
